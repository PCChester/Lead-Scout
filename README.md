# Lead Scout

A local AI-powered lead generation pipeline for finding companies actively investing in AI — and reaching out to them before anyone else does.

Built by an AI Adoption Trainer who got tired of manually searching LinkedIn.

---

## What It Does

Lead Scout finds companies showing real AI adoption signals, scores them for fit, classifies their type, tracks down a decision-maker's contact, and drafts a personalised cold email — all in one run. Results stream in live as each company clears the pipeline.

**The pipeline:**

1. **Company Discovery** — Tavily API searches for companies matching AI adoption queries. Industry-specific query sets for Fintech and Spanish/Valencia regions. Filters out job boards, aggregators, and noise domains, and deduplicates by domain
2. **AI Readiness Scoring** — Claude visits each company's public website and scores them 1–10 based on actual signals: blog posts, job listings, press releases, product pages. Supports multilingual content (Spanish, French, German, Dutch, Italian). Companies that merely *write about* AI (news sites, conferences, market research publishers) are disqualified. Large multinationals (500+ employees) are also disqualified in favour of SMEs and scale-ups
3. **Company Type Classification** — Each lead is classified as `client` (potential training/automation client), `competitor` (similar services to Chris), or `employer` (could hire Chris internally)
4. **Region & Role Classification** — Filters by geography (EU remote, US remote, Valencia in-person) so you're not chasing leads you can't take
5. **Contact Discovery** — Hunter.io finds the best decision-maker email at the company (Head of People, HR Director, CTO, etc.)
6. **Email Drafting** — Claude writes a short, warm, specific cold email. The angle adapts to company type: sales pitch for clients, job inquiry for competitors, team-building inquiry for employers

---

## How It Works

The app runs locally as a Flask web app. You pick an industry, region, and max cards, hit Scout, and the pipeline runs. Results don't wait for the full run to finish — they stream in via Server-Sent Events as each company clears scoring, so you're reading leads while it's still working.

The scoring stage is where most of the intelligence lives. Rather than keyword matching, Claude reads the actual website content and makes a judgment call: is this company genuinely adopting AI internally, or just publishing content about it? Anything that scores below 6 is dropped before it wastes your time.

Contacts come from Hunter.io's domain search. The pipeline prioritises decision-maker titles and rejects any result that resolves to a LinkedIn URL. Email drafts are generated by Claude with a system prompt that positions it as Chris — so the tone, context, and soft CTA are consistent every time. The email angle shifts automatically based on whether the company is a client prospect, a competitor, or a potential employer.

## What It Won't Do

- **No LinkedIn scraping** — against their ToS, and the data is noisy anyway
- **No storing contact databases** — GDPR. Contacts are surfaced per-session, not saved
- **No auto-sending emails** — you review and send manually, always
- **No fake emails** — any Hunter.io result containing `linkedin.com` is rejected automatically

---

## Known Limitations

- Card titles occasionally show page titles instead of company names (Tavily returning the article title rather than the company name)
- Hunter.io sometimes returns generic `info@` or "Hiring Team" contacts rather than named decision-makers — a coverage limitation on their end
- Occasional noise results still slip through the disqualify filter

These are tracked and will be addressed in a future tuning session using session log data.

---

## Why This Exists

Cold outreach works best when it's specific. Generic "I help companies with AI" emails go straight to trash. This tool forces specificity — every email references something real found on the company's website, and the angle is tailored to whether they're a client, a competitor, or a potential employer.

It's also a portfolio project demonstrating an end-to-end AI pipeline: search → scrape → score → classify → enrich → generate. Results stream in live. The whole thing runs locally with no database, no cloud infra, and no monthly SaaS bill.

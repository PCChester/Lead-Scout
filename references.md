### Example 1 — Competitor/Employer outreach (AI-native martech company)
**Why it works:**
- Opens with a specific, genuine observation about the company — not generic flattery
- Leads with experience but frames it around value to them, not a CV recitation
- "Making people comfortable enough to change how they work" is a strong, memorable hook
- CTA is low-pressure and specific (20-minute Zoom, not "let's connect")
- Warm but professional — reads like a human wrote it, not a template

**Original output (Lead Scout draft):**
Hi there,
I'm reaching out because Wellows caught my eye as an AI-native platform working in martech — exactly the kind of environment where I think my background could add real value. I'm Valencia-based with 30 years of experience helping people actually use what gets built: corporate communication and training across Europe and the US, now focused on AI adoption and workflow automation. I've trained teams at Mercadona, Microsoft Munich, and others — always bridging the gap between complex tools and the humans who need to trust them.
I'm curious whether you have (or foresee) any room for someone who can translate AI capabilities into something clients and internal teams actually understand and adopt. I've been hands-on with automation, prompt engineering, and building workflows — but my real strength is making people comfortable enough to change how they work.
If there's a fit worth exploring, I'd love a quick 20-minute call to hear more about where Wellows is headed.
Regards,
Chris

**Corrected version (send-ready standard):**
Hello [Company] [Team/Dept],

I'm reaching out because [Company] caught my eye as an AI-native platform working in [industry] — exactly the kind of environment where I think my background could add real value. I'm Valencia-based with 30 years of experience helping people communicate and work better together in international settings across Europe and the US, now focused on AI adoption and workflow automation. I've trained teams at Mercadona, Microsoft, and others — always bridging the gap between complex tools and the humans who need to trust them.

I'm curious whether you have (or foresee) any room for someone who can translate AI capabilities into something clients and internal teams actually understand and adopt. I've been hands-on with automation, prompt engineering, and building workflows — but my real strength is making people feel comfortable enough to change how they work.

If there's a fit worth exploring, I'd love a quick 20-minute Zoom call to hear more about where [Company] is headed and a possible collaboration.

Regards,
Chris
